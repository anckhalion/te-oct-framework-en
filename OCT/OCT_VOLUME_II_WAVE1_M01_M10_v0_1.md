# OCT Volume II - Wave 1 (M01-M10)
Version: v0.1Date: 2026-04-16Purpose: extended drafting of the first 10 classic expansion modules -> order.
Format for module:1. classical definition2. classical limit3. OCT extension4. candidate criterion/theorem5. validation
## M01 - Objects
Classic definition:the objects of a category are abstract entities linked by morphisms.
Classic limit:the object can be treated as a formally replaceable node in the presence of strong structural equivalences.
OCT Extension:l'oggetto e singolarita funzionale. Its identity and function in the field, not just syntactic position.
Criterion:two objects can be isomorphic but not orderly equivalent.
Validation:construct isomorphic pairs with different emergent profiles in the same diagrammatic dynamics.
## M02 - Morphisms
Classic definition:typed morphisms `f: A->B`.
Classic limit:typification and lawful composition do not imply generative value.
OCT Extension:a morphism is OCT-valid if it has generativity in the field and does not degrade coherence beyond the threshold.
Criterion:distinguish formally admitted morphisms from ordinally active morphisms.
Validation:morphism classification table on real cases (condoms, neutral, degenerative).
## M03 - Composition
Classic definition:`o` associativity and identity.
Classic limit:the composition is always definable when the types match.
OCT Extension:the composition remains definable, but the ordinal validity of the compound depends on `Coh`.
Criterion:theorem F03 (compositional closure consistent with stability hypothesis).
Validation:multi-stage pipeline with comparison between syntactic correctness and order stability.
## M04 - Identity
Classic definition:`id_A` neutral in composition.
Classic limit:only syntactic neutrality.
OCT Extension:`id_A` is ordinally neutral if it preserves singular function in the observational context.
Criterion:orderly neutrality of identity (F04, to be closed).
Validation:Identity iterations in dynamic systems and measurement of emergent drift.
## M05 - Isomorphisms
Classic definition:structural invertibility.
Classic limit:isomorphy tends to suggest full equivalence.
OCT Extension:Structural isomorphy does not imply orderly equivalence of role/function.
Criterion:F01 (non-collapse of the singularity).
Validation:constructive counterexamples with equal classical invariants and divergent `Phi`.
## M06 - Category equivalence
Classic definition:full, faithful, essentially surjective functor.
Classic limit:Formal equivalence can hide ontological loss.
OCT Extension:ordinal equivalence only if `Coh/Phi` invariants are also preserved.
Criterion:F07 (bound order equivalence).
Validation:pairs of classically-equivalent but not ordinally equivalent categories.
## M07 - Subcategories
Classic definition:selection of objects/morphisms from a parent category.
Classic limit:does not separate living from degenerative substructures.
OCT Extension:classification into live/degenerated subcategories via `Phi`.
Criterion:F08 (minimal non-degeneration).
Validation:map local system regions with the same syntactic degree and different vitality.
## M08 - Limits
Classic definition:universal object for cones.
Classic limit:universality does not guarantee functional production.
OCT Extension:order limit also requires emergent non-nullity.
Criterion:F05 (selective universality of limits).
Validation:cases in which the classical limit exists but does not exceed the ordering threshold.
## M09 - Colimiti
Classic definition:co-universal object for cocones.
Classic limit:formally valid aggregation may be sterile.
OCT Extension:ordering limit valid only if aggregation does not destroy coherence/function.
Criterion:F06 (selective universality of limits).
Validation:module/agent fusion experiments with pre/post measurement of `Coh` and `Phi`.
## M10 - Equalizers and co-equalizers
Classic definition:universal devices for comparing/quoting parallel arrows.
Classic limit:resolution formally correct but not necessarily significant in the field.
OCT Extension:valid equalization when it preserves singular function and systemic coherence.
Criterion:consistent equalization criterion (to be formalized in v0.2).
Validation:scenarios with narrative conflicts: same classic equalization, different ordering outcomes.
