# OCT Foundational Opera Architecture v0.1

Provisional title:
**Ordinative Category Theory - Integral Foundation of the Real Composition**

Objective:
build a foundational work on an OST scale that does not "apply" category theory to the OST,
but **systematically expands it** into a general ordering categorical theory.

## 1. Strong positioning

The OCT must be:
1. conservative on classical categorical syntax;
2. expansive on ontological semantics;
3. predictive on an operational level;
4. scientifically falsifiable.

Guide formula:
`Classical Category Theory + Ordinative Validity Layer = OCT`

## 2. Architecture of the work ("OST" scale)

## Volume I - Foundation

1. Crisis of extensional ontology and limits of pure form.
2. From thing to singularity.
3. Relational field and emergent function.
4. Canonical definition of ordinative category.
5. Axioms O1-O7 (rigorous version).
6. Reality, coherence, degeneration, internal observer.

Output: non-negotiable ontological-formal core.

## Volume II - Expansion of classical categorical theory

Chapter-by-chapter expansion of the entire classical grammar:

1. Objects, morphisms, identity, composition.
2. Isomorphisms, equivalences, full/faithful/essentially surjective.
3. Limits and colimits in an emergent way.
4. Functors, natural transformations, 2-categories.
5. Additions, monads, comonads.
6. Monoidal, closed, symmetric, braided categories.
7. Enriched categories.
8. Fibrate theory, indexed categories, observational context.
9. Topos and categorical logics with ordinative validity.
10. Categorical dynamics (processes, trajectories, attractors, Lyapunov).

Output: complete map "classic -> ordinative".

## Volume III - Theorems and validity criteria

1. Conservation theorems (what remains unchanged from classical).
2. Extension theorems (what emerges only in OCT).
3. Collapse/degeneration theorems.
4. Reconstruction theorems (adjunction-based recovery).
5. Observer and context theorems.

Output: distinctive theoretical corpus.

## Volume IV - Scientific methodology

1. OCT falsifiability protocol.
2. Metrics: `Coh`, `Phi`, `Delta`.
3. Multi-domain experimental design.
4. Benchmark against classical models.
5. Replicability criteria.

Output: entry into the grammar of contemporary human science.

## Volume V - Strong Applications

1. AI (generative models, alignment, agents).
2. Language and semantics.
3. Relational psychology.
4. Social and institutional systems.
5. Economy and geopolitical dynamics.
6. Epistemology and methodology of science.

Output: Demonstration of transdisciplinary breadth and utility.

## 3. Expansion matrix (core)

For each classic block, the OCT must explain:
1. Classical definition.
2. Classical limit.
3. Order extension.
4. New theorem or criterion.
5. Operational case.

Standard chapter format:
`Classical -> Limitation -> OCT Extension -> Theorem -> Validation`

## 4. Minimal theorem program

To avoid redundancy, OCT must produce at least:
1. 10 foundational theorems (with formal proof sketch).
2. 5 differential theorems (impossible in pure classical framework).
3. 3 application theorems with empirical protocol.

Without this block, the work remains philosophical; with this block, it becomes scientific theory.

## 5. Clear distinction OST vs OCT

OST:
- establishes ontology of set/field/function.

OCT:
- establishes universal compositional grammar of processes.
- makes the ontological validity internal to the categorical syntax.

Report:
`OST = fondazione ontologica`
`OCT = fondazione composizionale estesa`
