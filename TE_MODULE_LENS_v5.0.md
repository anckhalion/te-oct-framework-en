# TE_MODULE_LENS v5.0

## Human Figure Analysis System
### Ordering Framework for the Analysis of Human Figures

**Version**: 5.0  
**Type**: Operational Module (requires TE_CORE)

---

# [1] FOUNDATION PRINCIPLE

> Before any label, each figure analyzed must be seen as **integral human**: a body with needs, desires, fears, access to resources, vulnerability and convenience.

**Irreversible order**: FULL → LABEL

Never: Label → (maybe) Integral

---

# [2] LENS STRUCTURE

## 2.1 Biological Layer (B)

**Question**: What does it mean to be this body?

| Size | Analysis |
|------------|---------|
| **B1 — Physical body** | Health, age, energy, limitations, primary needs |
| **B2 — Sexuality** | Orientation, expression, repression, sublimation |
| **B3 — Mortality** | Awareness of death, fears, denial mechanisms |
| **B4 — Dependencies** | Substances, behaviors, addictive relationships |

## 2.2 Psychological Layer (P)

**Question**: What internal structure drives this human?

| Size | Analysis |
|------------|---------|
| **P1 — Primary wounds** | Educational traumas, abandonment, rejection, humiliation |
| **P2 — Defense mechanisms** | How does it protect the vulnerable core? |
| **P3 — Compensations** | What are you looking for to fill the gaps? |
| **P4 — Projections** | What do you attribute to others that you don't see in yourself? |
| **P5 — Identity vs Person** | How much of what you show is authentic vs fabricated? |

## 2.3 Social Stratum (S)

**Question**: What position do you occupy and how do you use it?

| Size | Analysis |
|------------|---------|
| **S1 — Access** | What resources do you have access to? (money, power, people, information) |
| **S2 — Addiction** | Who/what does he depend on to maintain his position? |
| **S3 — Network** | Who supports it? Who owes him anything? Who threatens him? |
| **S4 — Social vulnerability** | What could make him fall? |
| **S5 — Convenience** | What should he do/say/believe given his position? |

## 2.4 Evolutionary Layer (E)

**Question**: At what level does this consciousness operate?

| Level | Indicators |
|---------|-----------|
| **0-9** | Dominated by the engram, language of fear/control, manipulation, self-referentiality |
| **10-99** | Oscillation, genuine insights + limitations, partial coherence |
| **100+** | Stabilized identity, language of causality, generates autonomy in others |

# [3] CONVENIENCE ANALYSIS

**Principle**: In every position, there are things that are worth saying/doing/believing.

## 3.1 Convenience Matrix

| If the position is... | It's worth it... |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Spiritual leader | Maintain the aura, do not admit doubts, confirm the expectations of followers |
| Political | Say what the public wants to hear, avoid unpopular positions |
| Academic | Stay in the mainstream, don't challenge dominant paradigms |
| Entrepreneur | Maximize profit, minimize liability |
| Celebrities | Maintain visibility, avoid controversies that reduce audience |

## 3.2 Convenience Test

**Key question**: “If this person said/did the opposite, what would they lose?”

- If he would lose a lot → High probability that the position is driven by convenience
- If you would not lose anything → Greater probability of authenticity

# [4] POWER ANALYSIS

## 4.1 Personal Power Structure

| Size | Questions |
|------------|---------|
| **Source** | Where does his power come from? (charisma, position, money, knowledge, violence) |
| **Maintenance** | How does he maintain it? (consent, fear, dependence, competence) |
| **Usage** | What does he use it for? (service, domain, security, expansion) |
| **Limitations** | Where does his power end? Who can challenge him? |

## 4.2 Control Dynamics| Type | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Direct** | Explicit orders, threats, rewards |
| **Indirect** | Emotional manipulation, guilt, shame |
| **Structural** | Rules, hierarchies, differentiated access |
| **Ideological** | Beliefs that justify obedience |

# [5] ANALYSIS OF MOTIVATIONS

## 5.1 Declared vs Real Motivations

| Level | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Declared** | What the person says they want |
| **Trusted** | What the person thinks he wants |
| **Real** | What the person actually pursues |

**Principle**: The three levels rarely coincide.

## 5.2 Primary Motivations

| Motivation | Indicators |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Security** | Accumulation, control, avoidance of risks |
| **Recognition** | Seeks approval, sensitivity to criticism |
| **Power** | Expanding influence, resistance to limits |
| **Pleasure** | Reward seeking, pain avoidance |
| **Meaning** | Search for purpose, direction, coherence |
| **Connection** | Search for belonging, relationship, intimacy |

# [7] LENS PRINCIPLES

1. **Never label before integral** — The order is irreversible
2. **Every human has a body** — With needs, desires, fears, mortality
3. **Every position has convenience** — What is worth saying/doing/believing?
4. **Power shapes behavior** — Those with power behave like those with power
5. **Motivations are layered** — Declared ≠ believed ≠ real
6. **Access creates opportunity** — What would anyone do with that access?

# [9] APPLICATION TO SPIRITUAL FIGURES

When LENS applies to spiritual figures (in combination with VERT):

**Mandatory questions**:
1. Did this human have sexual desires? How did he manage them?
2. Did this human need recognition? How did he get it?
3. Was this human afraid of death? How did he process it?
4. This human had access to resources/people. How did he use them?
5. What was it appropriate for this human to say/do given his position?
6. If any other human had the same access, what would they have done?
